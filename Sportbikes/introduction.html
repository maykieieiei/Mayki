Introduction

What do you need to know?

Sportbikes also knows as supersport motorcycles are two-wheeled vehicles that are powered by the engine, known for their speed and build for high speed performances over comfort and safety. The main feature of the sportbikes are the high-performance suspension system, powerful brakes and especially their fast acceleration. A sports motorcycle, sports bike, or sport bike is a motorcycle designed and optimized for speed, acceleration, braking, and cornering on asphalt concrete race tracks and roads. They are mainly designed for performance at the expense of comfort, fuel economy, safety, noise reduction and storage in comparison with other motorcycles.

Sport bikes can be and are typically equipped with fairings and a windscreen to deflect wind from the rider to improve aerodynamics.

Top 10 Fastest Sportbikes

10. Ducati Panigale V4 R

Top Speed: 299 km/h

Price: 45,495 dollars / 2,650,077 pesos / 43,432

The Ducati Panigale V4 R was introduced in 2018. With the full racing exhaust and Shell Oil, the Panigale V4 R can produce 240.5 horsepower in closed course mode. Only closed-course circuit vehicles are designed to use the racing exhaust. The law forbids operation on public roadways.

With a maximum engine speed of 16,500 rpm in sixth gear (16,000 in other ratios) and a maximum power output of 207 horsepower at 13,500 rpm, the new 998 cc Desmosedici Stradale R is the engine at the heart of the Panigale V4 R, absolute benchmark values in the panorama of supersports bikes of the same displacement.

9. Aprilia RSV4 Factory

Top Speed: 305 km/h

Price: 25,999 dollars / 1,519,095 peso / 24,845 euro

The RSV4 solidifies its position as the industry leader in the superbike class with its exceptional performance. The Aprilia RSV4 is the first model in the versatile line; its performance and engine make it the pinnacle of technological perfection. Its brother bike, the Aprilia RSV4 Factory, has a more advanced setup that features Öhlins Smart EC semiactive suspension and forged aluminum wheel rims.

The 2025 Aprilia RSV4 is the most powerful production superbike in the world thanks to an upgraded E5+ V4 engine that produces 220 horsepower, new Brembo Hypure front callipers, winglets, and an inventive predictive system for electronic controls management. With Öhlins Smart EC 2.0 semi-active suspension that operates corner-by-corner and comes with Track Pack, Comfort Pack, and Race Pack as standard features, the Aprilia RSV4 Factory continues to be the undisputed leader of the pack.

7. BMW M 1000 RR

Top Speed: 314 km/h

Price: 39,149 dollars / 34,050 euro / 2,295,000 pesos

For everyone who cares about every instant. The racing-approved superbike has undergone aerodynamic optimization in a wind tunnel and additional development on the racetrack. The ShiftCam engine, which produces 205 horsepower, and the redesigned trim panel with aerodynamic winglets are responsible for the M RR's top speed of 189 mph. Even down to the smallest detail, this bike embodies performance, motorsport, and exclusivity. The BMW Motorrad Motorsport Race Support is no different.

6. Kawasaki Ninja H2

Top Speed: 322 km/h

Price: 28,147 dollars / 1,640,000 pesos / 27,209 euro

The Kawasaki Ninja H2 is a four-stroke supersport-class motorbike with a variable-speed centrifugal supercharger that is part of the Kawasaki Ninja sports bike line.

The fastest and most potent production motorbike available is the Ninja H2R track-only model, which can generate up to 310 horsepower (230 kW) and 326 horsepower (243 kW) with ram-air. Compared to the fastest street-legal motorcycles, the H2R has 50% greater power, whereas the street-legal Ninja H2 only produces 200 horsepower.
